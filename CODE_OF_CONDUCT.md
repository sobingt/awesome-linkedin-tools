# Community Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that our community will be harassment-free and that everyone will be treated with respect and dignity.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be respectful** - Value differing opinions and experiences
- **Be inclusive** - Welcome people of all backgrounds
- **Be collaborative** - Share knowledge and help others
- **Be professional** - Maintain a civil tone in all interactions
- **Focus on substance** - Discuss tool merits, not vendor marketing
- **Assume good intent** - Interpret ambiguous messages charitably

### Examples of Positive Behavior

- Using inclusive and welcoming language
- Providing constructive feedback with context
- Acknowledging other people's contributions
- Asking clarifying questions before dismissing suggestions
- Explaining technical decisions thoughtfully
- Helping newcomers get oriented

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are unacceptable:

- **Harassment** - Intimidation, threats, insults based on identity
- **Discrimination** - Exclusion based on race, gender, sexuality, religion, ability, etc.
- **Trolling** - Intentionally disruptive or provocative comments
- **Spam** - Unsolicited promotion or off-topic content
- **Bad faith arguments** - Deliberately misrepresenting positions
- **Doxxing** - Sharing private information without consent
- **Sexual content** - Inappropriate or unwelcome sexual comments/imagery

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- GitHub issues and pull requests
- Discussions and comments
- Email communications
- Any official community spaces

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it:

**Email**: Maintainer contact (add email or link to GitHub profile)  
**GitHub**: Report via issue with label `code-of-conduct-violation`

**Include**:
- Description of what happened
- Date/time if applicable
- People involved (if safe to share)
- Any evidence (screenshots, links)

Reports will be handled confidentially and promptly.

### Consequences

Community maintainers will investigate reports and determine appropriate responses:

1. **Warning** - Private message outlining the issue
2. **Comment removal** - Delete problematic content
3. **Temporary ban** - Restricted access for 30+ days
4. **Permanent ban** - Removal from community

## Our Responsibility

Maintainers are responsible for:
- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate action for unacceptable behavior
- Removing or editing comments/contributions that violate the Code
- Protecting the confidentiality of reporters
